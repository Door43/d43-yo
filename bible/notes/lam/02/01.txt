===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **Daughter of Zionâ€¦Daughter of Judah**  - Cities were commonly pictured as women. (See: [[:yo:ta:vol2:translate:figs_personification|Personification]])
  * **The writer uses metaphors to express God's anger and judgment.**  (See: [[:yo:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor|Metaphor]])
      * **How the Lord has covered the daughter of Zion with a dark cloud**  - The writer uses weather to picture God's angry intentions.
      * **He has cast down the beauty of Israel from heaven to the earth**  - paralellism to following metaphor-This refers to Judah losing her position of influence and favor with God to a low position exposed to destruction.
      * "not remembered his footstool" - paralellism with the previous metaphor - A footstool in this context is Judah's former favored position.  To be forgotten is to be left exposed and with out help.
      * **thrown down the fortified cities **  - This refers to the destruction of Judah' key cities for the defense of their country.
      * At: "God has destroyed Judah's defenses" (using Judah's enemies to accomplish this).
      * **struck them down to the ground**  -This refers to the Babylonians defeating Judah in war and taking the royal family captive.
