===== Translation Notes:** words** - explanation =====

  * **The writer continues the use of metaphors to express god's opposition to Judah.**
  * "he has cut off all the strength of Israel" God has removed all the physical resources that Judah had previous.
  * "drawn back his right hand" This phrase refers to god's help intentionally being pull back to expose judah to harm.
  * "burned up Jacob" This is both a literal and figurative image of judah as devastated.
  * "bent his bow at us like an enemy" - This image poetically expresses god's seeming hostile posture towards Judah.
  * "stood in battle positon as an adversary" -God's posture seems to have changed sides to opose Judah like an enemy would..
  * "he has slaughtered"- This metaphor is both literal and figurative.
  * "poured out his wrath like fire" - The writer uses this metaphor to illustrate the destructive intensity of god's actions.
  * Theological note: The writer views God as using judah's enemies as his own weapon against judah.
